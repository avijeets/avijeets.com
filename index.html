About Me

I’m currently a student at Rutgers University studying Computer Science, Philosophy, and Cognitive Science. I first got into programming writing functions on AIM away messages and writing Javascript popup messages to annoy every Internet user ever. This eventually led to my passion for technology and teaching code. I eventually would create the Computer Science Hub, a room for constant programming help and an after-school club for pair-programming, and make a competitive VEX Robotics team in my high school while traveling to New York City on weekends to mentor a group of kids at CoderDojo NYC.

Aside from teaching programming, I’ve worked on a couple interesting projects and held internships at great companies. At Rutgers University now, I help out with HackRU and USACS, work on RuMAD, dance on Rutgers Bhangra, and serve as a webmaster and brother of Delta Sigma Iota, Inc.
